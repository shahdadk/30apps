Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 11, 2026 — applies to all apps published by Shahdad Kompanizare on the App Store.

Short version

These apps collect nothing about you. No analytics. No tracking. No advertising. No account. No network requests of any kind unless you explicitly tap a link to an external website.

What runs on your device, and stays there

Some apps save your settings or recent results so the app remembers them between launches — examples: your last selected unit in CookConvert, your tap history in TapBPM, your favorite quote in Stoic Pocket, your last cocktail viewed in Cocktail Deck. All of this lives in your device's local storage (UserDefaults) and never leaves your device. Deleting the app removes this data.

What permissions the apps use

  • HikeWhistle uses the device flashlight (no camera capture, no recording) so it can strobe a visual signal in time with the safety whistle.
  • Morse Dot uses the system audio output to play Morse code tones, and optionally the flashlight (same as above) for silent visual practice.
  • No app in this collection asks for location, microphone, camera, contacts, photos, motion, health, or notifications.

Third parties

There are no third-party SDKs in any of these apps. No analytics. No crash reporters. No advertising networks. Apple's standard App Store privacy nutrition label for every app in this collection reads "Data Not Collected."

Children

These apps are appropriate for general audiences, and most do not require any age gating. Several apps (Cocktail Deck) are rated 17+ because they describe alcoholic recipes. Cipher Kit, PigLatinizer, and PeriodicTap are designed in part for younger learners.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the "Last updated" line above will change with it. The change history is visible in the public repository at the bottom of this page.
